* Introduction
"The C Programming Language" by Kernighan and Ritchie is considered a rite of
passage for *all* programmers, no matter background. This site serves as a log of
my journey through that path, and represents my "mark" at the end along with
countless others and even more to come.

Less poetically, K&R is the text I am using to prepare for the "Programming in
C" course in the second semester of my Computer Science education (and primarily
to learn C for my own use). The semester starts end of February of this year,
I'm hoping I'll finish the book end to end by April? Hopefully that prediction
ages well.
* Local Setup
As of January 2026, I'm running Fedora, so I'll be using GCC and GNUmake as my
compiler and build tool. I attempt exercises in Emacs Org files, but I'm yet to
decide whether Org Babel or a manual tangle-and-compile meets my needs better.
